Know Before the Flames

Here are some of the most frequently asked questions about our fire department services, emergency procedures, and community programs.

How do I get a copy of a fire report?

Fire reports can be obtained by visiting our main station during business hours or by submitting a written request. You'll need to provide the incident date, location, and your identification. There may be a small processing fee for copies.

Can I have a campfire or burn yard debris in my area?

Burning regulations vary by location and season. Please contact our fire prevention office to check current burn restrictions and obtain necessary permits. We also provide guidelines for safe burning practices.

How can I become a firefighter?

We offer both volunteer and career firefighter opportunities. Visit our volunteer page to learn about requirements and apply. Career positions are posted when available and require completion of our training academy.

Do you offer fire extinguisher training for businesses or groups?

Yes, we provide fire safety education and extinguisher training for businesses, schools, and community groups. Contact our fire prevention office to schedule a training session for your organization.

Emergency & Safety Questions

How do I report a fire or emergency?

For all emergencies, dial 911 immediately. Provide the dispatcher with your location, the nature of the emergency, and any other relevant information. Stay on the line until the dispatcher tells you it's okay to hang up.

Can I volunteer with the fire department?

Absolutely! We welcome volunteers who are committed to serving their community. You must be at least 18 years old, pass a background check, and complete our training program. Visit our volunteer page for more information.

How does the department respond to emergencies?

When we receive an emergency call, our dispatch center immediately alerts the appropriate units. Our goal is to have the first unit on scene within 6 minutes for fire emergencies and 4 minutes for medical emergencies in our primary response area.

Do you offer fire safety education or training?

Yes, we offer various fire safety programs including school visits, business training, CPR classes, and community education events. Contact our fire prevention office to schedule a program for your group.

How often should I check my smoke detectors?

Test your smoke detectors monthly and replace batteries at least once a year. We recommend changing batteries when you change your clocks for daylight saving time. Replace smoke detectors every 10 years.
